d:T6b7,<p>Under market surveillance according to Regulation (EU) 2019/1020, the market surveillance authority is empowered</p>
<ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mt-4">
<li>under Article 14.4 a-14.4 c, to demand that economic operators provide documents, specifications, data, or information,</li>
<li>under Article 14.4 d, to conduct unannounced inspections on-site and physical product checks,</li>
<li>under Article 14.4 e, to access premises, land, or vehicles,</li>
<li>under Article 14.4 f, to initiate investigations on its own initiative,</li>
<li>under Article 14.4 g, to require economic operators to take appropriate measures to cease non-compliance or eliminate a risk,</li>
<li>under Article 14.4 h, to take action itself,</li>
<li>under Article 14.4 j, to acquire, inspect, and dismantle samples of goods, and</li>
<li>under Article 14.4 k, to require the removal of content on an online interface, display a warning, or restrict access.</li>
</ol> <p>The market surveillance authority may procure a sample of goods under a hidden identity according to Article 14.4 j of Regulation (EU) 2019/1020 only if necessary to achieve the purpose of the inspection. The authority must inform the economic operator that the procurement was done under a hidden identity, as soon as possible without compromising the purpose of the action.</p> <p>The authority's powers under item 8 of the first paragraph do not apply to databases covered by the protections of the Freedom of the Press Act or the Freedom of Expression Act.</p> <p>When changed circumstances warrant it, the market surveillance authority shall decide that such an obligation referred to in item 8 of the first paragraph shall no longer apply.
